Global Warming?

This graph illustrates the change in global surface temperature relative to 1951-1980 average temperatures. The 10 warmest years in the 134-year record all have occurred since 2000, with the exception of 1998. The year 2015 ranks as the warmest on record. (Source: NASA/GISS). This research is broadly consistent with similar constructions prepared by the Climatic Research Unit and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (nasa.gov).

What?

Global warming is the gradual increase in the average temperature of both the Earth's atmosphere and its oceans.

Who?

Most scientists looking into recent temperature patterns and facts agree the planet is warming, a change that is believed to be permanently changing the Earth’s climate.

When?

Overall, the average temperature of the Earth has risen between 0.4 and 0.8 °C over the past 100 years.

Why?

The increased volumes of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases produced by human activities, are commonly known as the primary sources of global warming for the past 50 years.

So?

These changes could result in rising sea levels due to the melting of the polar ice caps, as well as an increase in occurrence and severity of severe weather events.

Global Warming Factors

What's actually going on?

Electricity Consumption: In 2005 alone, a staggering 86 percent of energy used worldwide was produced by the combustion of fossil fuels.


Carbon Dioxide Emissions: Unfortunately, fossil fuels are also the primary culprit behind climate change. Natural processes can absorb some of this CO2, approximately 4.1 billion metric tons of C02 are added to our atmosphere annually.


GDP: As countries become more industrialized, the demand for energy consumption increases. By default, the rate of C02 emissions increases and contributes to both pollution rates and increasing temperature worldwide.

Thinking Forward...

So what now?

The Problem

Greenhouse gas concentrations will continue to increase unless the billions of tons of our annual emissions decrease significantly. These changes will negatively impact our food sources, water supply, ecosystems, infrastructure, and our own health.

The Solution

Solar power plants, wind mills, and hydraulic dams are all clean alternatives to energy production. Not only are they sources of renewable energy, but they also reduce the amount of pollutants released in the atmosphere.

The Plan

Nations around the world joining the fight against climate change. At the Paris climate summit in 2015, 195 countries signed a treaty to reduce their carbon emissions and limit temperature change.

The Future

Though you might feel like your lifestyle is insignificant to Global Warming, the choices we make — how we get around, what we eat, how we live — play a major role in slowing climate change. Click here to learn more!
